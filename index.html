Crimes and Prisoners in the U.S.A (2016)

Prisoner Count and Quantity of Crimes per state

Below are a choropleth map, and a heat map with the choropleth map showing the number of prisoners in holding around each state of America, and the heat map showing the quantities of each crime type occuring in each state in 2016. As shown in the heatmap Oklahoma has the highest average number of convicts in 2016 with 67 convicts per 10,000 people. In the heatmap, it is evident that Aggravated Assault is the most common form of violent crime to occur in the U.S.A, whereas Murder Manslaughter is the least common.

Division of Crimes and Conviction Rate

Displayed on the left are two bar charts, the Division of Crimes stacked bar chart, and the Violent Crimes vs Population bar chart. The divison of crimes chart displays the total number of violent crimes occuring in each state around the U.S.A in 2016. As well as the split of how each different crime type contributes to the total.

From the Division of Crimes chart, it the most prevalent crime type is Aggravated Assault, followed by Robbery, Rape and Murder Manslaughter respectively. This statistic is led by California with a total of 174,769 violent crimes occuring throughout 2016.

In the Violent Crimes vs Population bar chart, the average amount of crimes occuring per 10,000 people is shown. Alaska has the highest overall crimes occuring out of all other states with overall 80 violent crimes per 10,000 people in 2016. This is closely followed by New Mexico, Nevada and Tennessee with 70, 68 and 64 violent crimes occuring per 10,000 people respectively.

Crimes and Prisoners

Displayed on the left is scatter plot, depicting the states with the top 10 violent crime totals in the U.S.A in 2016. The visualisation highlights the relationship that exists between the the number of violent crimes commited, and the number of prisoners in holding in each state.

As shown, California has the highest number of violent crimes commited at a total of 174,796. Texas has the highest total number of prisoners in holding at 151,276 prisoners. Florida is also worth taking note of, as it is not in the same cluster as the other states, with 88,700 violent crimes comitted and 98,010 prisoners in holding.
